About Anubhav

Anubhav is a grassroots non-profit collective of around 25 dedicated members. We work with tribal and distressed families in hard-to-reach areas of West Bengal, India. Our mission is simple: be physically present where others cannot go, and provide meaningful, respectful support.

Who we are

We started Anubhav after witnessing how entire villages in remote forest areas and river islands were missing from most development efforts. Roads are broken, mobile networks are weak, and without local guidance, outsiders cannot even find some of the hamlets.

Our team includes volunteers and local community members who guide us safely through the jungles and river routes of:

  • Jhargram
  • Bankura
  • Purulia
  • Sundarbans (river island villages)

With a modest annual budget of around ₹1.5 lakh, we prioritise direct support to families and children, keeping administrative costs low and community ownership high.

What makes us different

Last-Mile Access

Our strength is in deep reach. We go beyond the main roads, using local knowledge to connect with families who rarely, if ever, receive support from formal systems or larger NGOs.

Local Relationships

We work with the community, not just for them. Villagers guide us, help identify the most distressed families, and assist in organising distributions and educational activities.

Our Work

We focus on simple, concrete interventions that immediately improve the lives of children, elders and vulnerable families in remote tribal and riverine communities.

Food Support

In times of distress, natural calamity or acute need, we provide basic food support to families in remote hamlets. Our team carries essentials across difficult terrain to reach those who have little or no access to markets and relief.

Clothing & Festival Support

Around Durga Puja and other key times of the year, we distribute clothes for children and elders so that even the most marginalised families can celebrate with dignity. For many children, this is the only new dress they receive all year.

Blankets & Winter Relief

Winters can be harsh for elders and small children living in poorly insulated homes. We distribute warm blankets in interior villages, prioritising elderly people and families in the most difficult conditions.

Education Support

We run a tuition centre for 70 children on a Sundarbans island, helping them stay in school and build strong foundations in basic subjects. This small but dedicated space gives children a safe place to learn, ask questions and dream of a different future.

Sundarbans Island Tuition Centre

On a river island in the Sundarbans, far from city facilities, 70 children gather for daily tuition classes supported by Anubhav. Your contribution keeps this lifeline of learning open.

Why this school matters

Children in the Sundarbans face multiple challenges: long distances to formal schools, frequent flooding and cyclones, economic pressure on families, and a lack of quiet, safe study spaces.

Our tuition centre provides:

  • Daily support in core subjects
  • Guidance for homework and exams
  • A safe and encouraging environment
  • Attention for children who are at risk of dropping out

We work closely with parents and local leaders to keep education a priority, even when times are tough.

Stories from the Field

Names and details changed for privacy. Photos used with consent where applicable.

In a small hamlet deep inside the forest in Jhargram, an elderly widow lived alone in a mud house with a leaking roof and no warm bedding. Every winter, she would sit awake most of the night, unable to sleep in the cold. With the support of our donors, we were able to bring her a thick blanket and some basic food essentials. When our team visited her a month later, she told us, “This is the first winter in many years when I have slept peacefully.”

– Field visit, Jhargram
